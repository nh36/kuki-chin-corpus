# Literature Review: Interrogatives in Tedim Chin

This report compiles previous scholarship on interrogative constructions in Tedim Chin.

---

## 1. Henderson (1965)

Henderson does not provide a dedicated section on interrogatives. Her texts are primarily narrative and contain few questions. The question particle system is not systematically described.

---

## 2. Zam Ngaih Cing (2017/2018)

### 2.1 Question Particles (§7.3.15)

> "The question particle in Tedim Chin occurs at the end of the sentence. It can also occur with wh-question which occurs at the beginning of the sentence."

```
nà    paī-út        hí-á
2SG   go.1-want.1   COP-Q
'Do you want to go?'

kɔí-àɁ     téŋ      ná    hī-ā
where-LOC  live.1   2SG   COP-Q
'Where do you live?'
```

### 2.2 Interrogative Types (§7.4.1)

> "Interrogative clause is of two types: yes-no questions and question word questions."

#### 2.2.1 Yes-No Questions (§7.4.1.1)

> "In yes-no question, the answer is expected to be given with 'yes' or 'no'. It is also called polar interrogatives (Hartmann 2009: 310)."

```
nà    pá       tʰɔú-tà    m
2POSS father   get.up-PFV Q
'Has your father got up already?'
```

Polite response repeats part of the question:
```
hè    kà      pá       tʰɔú-tà
yes   2POSS   father   get.up-PFV
'Yes, my father has got up already'
```

#### 2.2.2 Intonation (§7.4.1.2)

> "Intonation is used to mark various moods of the sentence. The function of intonation is to raise a question."

**High abrupt pitch** = speaker is unhappy OR confirming known information:
```
paī   mɔ̀
go.1  Q
'Did you go/You go?' (with abrupt high pitch)
```

**Level pitch** = neutral confirmation:
```
paī   m
go.1  Q
'You go?' (with level pitch)
```

#### 2.2.3 Interrogative Particles (§7.4.1.3)

| Formal | Informal | Function |
|--------|----------|----------|
| hiam | hia | General question |
| diam | dia | Irrealis question (< díŋ + hiam) |

> "'hia' and 'dia' are more casual and used in informal occasion whereas 'hiam' and 'diam' are more formal."

**diam** combines irrealis + question, functions as request:
```
vɔ̀k-té   án    nà    piá     diàm
pig-PL   food  2SG   give.1  PURP-Q
'Will you feed the pigs?'
```

#### 2.2.4 Tag Questions (§7.4.1.4)

> "Tag question is a form of yes/no question... The expected response from the other person is an affirmative answer."

```
náŋ   ciàʔ        díŋ    hì-màʔ    lɔù     ná    hí-á
2SG   return.home PURP   COP.EMPH  NEG.1   2SG   COP-Q
'You are going home, aren't you?'
```

#### 2.2.5 Question Words (§7.4.1.5)

Three basic question words with derivatives:

| Base | Gloss | Derived | Gloss |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| bàŋ | 'what' | bàŋháŋ | 'why' |
| kuà | 'who' | kuāá | 'whose' |
| | | kuàn | 'whom' |
| kɔí | 'which' | kɔíàɁ | 'where' |
| cìkciáŋ | 'when' | — | — |

### 2.3 Wh-Question Position and Verb Stem (§7.4.1.5)

> "With regard to the position, the question word occurs in situ or it can move to the front like most OV languages in the world. When the question word is in situ, the verb is in Stem 1 and when the question word is in sentence initial position, the verb is in Stem 2."

**In-situ** (Stem 1):
```
náŋ   tú     zīŋsāŋ     bàŋ    né
2SG   now    morning    what   eat.1
'What did you eat this morning?'
```

**Fronted** (Stem 2):
```
tú    zīŋsāŋ     bàŋ-à    nà    nék
now   morning    what-Q   2SG   eat.2
'What did you eat this morning?'
```

## 3. Singh (2018): Sukte Grammar [Comparative]

### 3.1 Question Formation Strategies (§8.1.3)

> "The interrogative clause in Sukte is mainly formed by using question word or question particle. However the interrogative clause is also formed by the raising intonation."

### 3.2 Question Words (§8.1.3.1)

| Sukte | Gloss |
|-------|-------|
| bəŋ | 'what' |
| kuə | 'who' |
| koi-əʔ | 'where' |
| bəŋhun | 'when' |

```
nəŋ    kuə    nə    hiam?
you    who    2SG   QP
'Who are you?'

əməʔ-in   bəŋ   sem    ə    hiam?
he-NOM    what  do     3SG  QP
'What does he do?'
```

### 3.3 Question Particles (§8.1.3.2)

> "In Sukte, the interrogative clause is also formed by the question particles **hiam**, **diam** etc. It is important to note that the particle **diam** is mutually derived from **diŋ** 'will/shall' and **hiam** 'question particle'."

```
huətak   əʔ     kə    pai    diam?
there    LOC    1SG   go     QM
'Shall I go there?'

nəŋ   saŋsia    nə    hiam?
you   teacher   2SG   QM
'Are you teacher?'
```

### 3.4 Rising Intonation (§8.1.3.3)

> "Like most of the languages in the world, the yes-no interrogative clause in Sukte can also be formed from the declarative sentences by using the rising intonation."

```
nəŋ   zatui      nə    ne    hi
you   medicine   2SG   eat   DECL
'You eat the medicine?' (with rising intonation)
```

## 4. Synthesis

### 4.1 Question Particle Cognates

| Function | Tedim | Sukte |
|----------|-------|-------|
| General Q | hiam / hia | hiam |
| Irrealis Q | diam / dia | diam |
| Simple Q | m / mɔ̀ | — |

### 4.2 Question Word Cognates

| Gloss | Tedim | Sukte |
|-------|-------|-------|
| 'what' | bàŋ | bəŋ |
| 'who' | kuà | kuə |
| 'where' | kɔíàɁ | koi-əʔ |
| 'when' | cìkciáŋ | bəŋhun |
| 'why' | bàŋháŋ | — |

### 4.3 Question Formation Strategies

| Strategy | Tedim | Sukte |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Question particle | ✓ | ✓ |
| Rising intonation | ✓ | ✓ |
| Wh-fronting | ✓ (with Stem 2) | — |
| Wh-in-situ | ✓ (with Stem 1) | ✓ |
| Tag questions | ✓ | — |

### 4.4 Distinctive Findings

**Zam Ngaih Cing's unique contributions**:
1. **Verb stem alternation** tied to wh-position (Stem 1 in-situ vs. Stem 2 fronted)
2. **Formal/informal register** distinction (hiam/diam vs. hia/dia)
3. **Intonation contrasts**: abrupt high vs. level pitch
4. **Tag question** formation with emphatic + negative

**Singh's contribution**:
- Confirms cognate question particles across Kuki-Chin

## 5. Open Questions

2. **Pitch contrasts**: How systematic is the intonation-meaning mapping?
3. **Focus**: Does wh-fronting involve focus movement?
