# Literature Review: Demonstratives in Tedim Chin

This report compiles previous scholarship on demonstrative pronouns and adverbs in Tedim Chin.

---

## 1. Henderson (1965)

Henderson does not provide a dedicated section on demonstratives, but examples in her texts show the basic forms:

- **hih** 'this' (proximal)
- **tua** 'that' (distal)

These appear in various constructions throughout her analyzed texts.

---

## 2. Zam Ngaih Cing (2017/2018)

### 2.1 Basic Forms (§3.2.3)

| Form | Gloss | Distance |
|------|-------|----------|
| híʔ [≈hih] | 'this' | Proximal |
| huā | 'that' | Distal |

> "A demonstrative pronoun occupies the pre-head modifying position in a noun phrase. It is used to point out something (object) from the rest of the entity."

### 2.2 Plural Formation

Plural demonstratives are formed by suffixing -té [≈-te]:

- (100) `híʔ [≈hih]-té [≈-te]` 'these'
- (101) `huā-té [≈-te]` 'those'

> "There is no separate plural demonstrative pronoun. The plurals are formed by the affixation of the plural marker -té [≈-te] to the singular demonstratives."

### 2.3 Locative Demonstratives

Locative forms are derived from demonstrative + progressive + locative:

| Base | Derived | Contracted | Gloss |
|------|---------|------------|-------|
| híʔ [≈hih] | híɁ [≈hih]-laī-áɁ [≈-ah] | hiáɁ | 'here' |
| huā | huā-laī-áɁ [≈-ah] | huáɁ | 'there' |

### 2.4 Syntactic Position

Demonstratives occupy the **pre-head modifying position** in the NP:

- (104) `híɁ [≈hih] sám [≈sam]-zāŋ [≈zang]...` "this hair-strand..."

### 2.5 Discourse Functions (§3.3.5.1)

Demonstratives function in discourse deixis:

> "The demonstrative pronouns híʔ [≈hih] 'this' and huā 'that' are derived from the demonstrative pronouns and the locative marker -àʔ [≈-ah]."

## 3. Singh (2018): Sukte Grammar [Comparative]

### 3.1 Basic Forms (§4.6.2)

| Form | Gloss | Distance |
|------|-------|----------|
| hiʔ [≈hih] | 'this' | Proximal |
| huə [≈hua] | 'that' | Distal |
| hiʔte [≈hihte] | 'these' | Proximal plural |
| huəte [≈huate] | 'those' | Distal plural |

> "The Demonstrative pronoun may be classified in terms of place or in terms of the speaker's attitude. Because of this distinction (as in Huddleston 1984: 296) called proximal and distal demonstrative pronoun."

---

## 4. Synthesis

### 4.1 Two-Way Distance Contrast

All sources document a simple **proximal-distal** contrast:

| | Proximal | Distal |
|---|----------|--------|
| Henderson | hih | tua |
| Zam Ngaih Cing | híʔ [≈hih] | huā |
| Sukte (Singh) | hiʔ [≈hih] | huə [≈hua] |

### 4.2 Cognate Forms

The demonstrative forms are clearly cognate across Kuki-Chin:
- Proximal: **hi-** (PTB origin)
- Distal: **tua/hua** (variant transcriptions of same form)

### 4.3 Grammatical Properties

| Property | Tedim | Sukte |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Position | Pre-nominal | Pre-nominal |
| Plural marker | -té [≈-te] | -te |
| Locative derivation | DEM + -laī-áɁ [≈-ah] | Not documented |

## 5. Open Questions

1. **Three-way deixis?**: Is there evidence for a medial demonstrative (visible but not near)?
2. **Anaphoric use**: How are demonstratives used for discourse anaphora?
3. **Adnominal vs. pronominal**: Any formal distinction between adnominal and pronominal use?
