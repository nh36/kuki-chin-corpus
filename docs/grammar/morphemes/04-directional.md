## Overview: Directional Marking in Tedim Chin

Tedim Chin has a rich system of directional marking that operates in two distinct slots:

| Position | Function | Markers |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Pre-verbal | Deictic reference (speaker-oriented) | hong-/ong- "toward", va- "away", ma- "forward" |
| Post-verbal | Elevational/spatial motion | -toh "up", -suk "down", -phei "horizontal", -lam "toward" |

This file focuses on the **post-verbal directional suffixes**.

### Morphological Template Position

From ZNC (2018: §5.8.2):
> Verb [+ NEGATION] [+ CAUSATIVE] [+ DIRECTIONAL] [+ ADVERBIAL PARTICLES]

Directional suffixes occupy position 3-4 in the post-verbal derivational chain.

## -toh / -tawh (UP)

### Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Form | -toh (ZNC: -tɔù) |
| Category | Post-verbal directional suffix |
| Function | Upward motion |
| Semantic domain | Elevational |

### ZNC (2017/2018: Part 2, §5.8.2.3, p. 162)

**Classification:**
> "Verbal suffixes are used to denote three types of directions- upward, downward and horizontal (level)."

**EXAMPLE (362):**
`kah-toh`
[≈Bible: kah-toh]
climb-UPWARD
"ascend upward"

**Elevation system:**
ZNC describes an elevation-based opposition:
- Upward = northward (higher elevation)
- Downward = southward (lower elevation)

This reflects Myanmar geography where rivers flow south (downhill).

## Pre-Verbal Directionals

For completeness, the pre-verbal directional markers are documented here:

### hong- / ong- (VENT)

Venitive marker: motion toward speaker or speech-act location.

**ZNC (2018: §5.8.1.3):**
- Form: ɔ́ŋ- / hɔ́ŋ-
- Function: "towards" (goal directed towards speaker)
- Position: Pre-verbal

### va- (AND)

Andative marker: motion away from speaker.

**ZNC (2018: §5.8.1.3):**
- Form: và-
- Function: "away" (away from speaker)
- Position: Pre-verbal

**Co-occurrence (ZNC p. 161):**
When both hong- and va- occur, hong- ALWAYS precedes va-.

**EXAMPLE (344):**
`van-a aksinelkai hong va lak-sak nung ci`
[≈Bible: van-a aksinelkai hong va lak-sak nung ci]
"They said, 'We will go and get comet from the sky'"

### ma- (FORW)

Forward/advance marker.

**ZNC (2018: §5.8.1.3):**
- Form: mā-
- Function: "forward/advance" (literal: 'front')
- Position: Pre-verbal

## Cross-Source Synthesis

### Confirmed System (from ZNC 2018)

| Suffix | Gloss | Meaning | Position |
|--------|-------|---------|----------|
| -toh | UP | Upward motion | Post-verbal |
| -suk | DOWN | Downward motion | Post-verbal |
| -phei | HORIZ | Horizontal motion | Post-verbal |
| -lam | TOWARD | Goal direction | Post-verbal |
| hong- | VENT | Toward speaker | Pre-verbal |
| va- | AND | Away from speaker | Pre-verbal |
| ma- | FORW | Forward | Pre-verbal |

### Semantic Organization

The directional system encodes:

1. **Elevational axis** (three-way):
   - UP (-toh)
   - DOWN (-suk)
   - HORIZONTAL (-phei)

2. **Deictic reference** (two-way):
   - TOWARD speaker (hong-/ong-)
   - AWAY from speaker (va-)

3. **Goal orientation**:
   - TOWARD goal (-lam)
   - FORWARD (ma-)

## Open Questions

1. **-toh vs -tawh:** Is the comitative postposition -tawh etymologically related to directional -toh?

2. **Lexicalized compounds:** How many apparent "directional suffixes" are actually lexicalized compounds with former motion verbs?

3. **Productivity:** Which directionals are fully productive vs. restricted to certain verb classes?

4. **Aspectual extensions:** Do any directionals have grammaticalized aspectual meanings (e.g., -toh for completive, -suk for inception)?

5. **Analyzer inventory:** Should -suk and -phei be added to the analyzer? Should questionable items (-khia, -cip, -tang, -sawn) be removed or reclassified?
