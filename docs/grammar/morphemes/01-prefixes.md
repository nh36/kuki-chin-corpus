# Prefixes: Literature Database

This document compiles what previous scholarship says about each prefix in Tedim Chin, organized morpheme-by-morpheme with full citations and examples.

**Sources:**
- Henderson (1965) = Eugénie J.A. Henderson, "Tiddim Chin: A Descriptive Analysis of Two Texts"
- ZNC (2017/2018) = Zam Ngaih Cing & Temsunungsang, "A Grammar of Tedim Chin" Parts 1-2

## Overview: Prefix System in Tedim Chin

Tedim Chin has two main prefix classes:

| Class | Prefixes | Function |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Pronominal | ka-, na-, a-, i- | Person/possession marking |
| Directional | kong-, hong- | Object/directional marking |

### Morphological Template

```
[PRONOMINAL] + [DIRECTIONAL] + VERB-ROOT + [SUFFIXES]
```

Example: `kong-hong-pai-sak` 1→2-toward-go-CAUS "cause me to come to you"

## Pronominal Prefixes

### Overview

| Prefix | Person | Number | Function |
|--------|--------|--------|----------|
| ka- | 1 | SG/PL.EXCL | 1st person |
| na- | 2 | SG/PL | 2nd person |
| a- | 3 | SG/PL | 3rd person |
| i- | 1 | PL.INCL | 1st inclusive |

### Henderson (1965: pp. 32-33, 50-51, 58-59)

**Section D: "Pronominal concord within the sentence"**

Henderson provides the foundational analysis of pronominal prefixes as AGREEMENT markers.

**Direct quote (pp. 32-33):**
> "For all nominal forms except 'kei, keimah', &c., I, 'nang, nangmah', &c., you, 'ei, eite', &c., we, 'ko, kote', &c., we, 'no, note', &c., you, concord obtains with the pronominal prefix 'a-'. For the words listed above, the concordant pronominal prefixes are 'ka-, na-, i-, 'ka-', and 'na-' respectively."

**Paradigm (from Henderson):**

| Pronoun | Prefix | Person/Number |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| kei | ka- | 1SG |
| nang | na- | 2SG |
| ei | i- | 1PL.INCL |
| ko | ka- | 1PL.EXCL |
| no | na- | 2PL |
| amah | a- | 3SG/PL |

**Phonological properties (pp. 58-59):**
> "Prefixes, whether pronominal or verbal, are always lightly stressed in relation to the following word."

- Light stress relative to root
- Reduced vowel quality
- Binding stress between prefix and following word
- High-low pitch contour linking prefix to root

**Example sentences:**
- `ka pai` "I go" (1SG prefix + verb)
- `a inn` "his house" (3SG prefix + noun)
- `a kuan` "he go.work" (3SG agreement with verb)

**Functional role:**
- Mark agreement between subject nominal and predicate
- Function at SENTENCE level (across phrase boundaries)
- Conditioned by mood (present in indicative, variable in subjunctive)

### ZNC (2017/2018: §3.3.4.1.1, pp. 108-109)

**Conceptualization:**
> "The most common form of nominal prefix in Tedim Chin and Kuki-Chin languages (Henderson 1965; Chhangte 1989) in general is a pronominal prefix. It is a prefix where first, second and third person singular and plural forms are used before a noun."

**Examples with IPA:**

**(181) 1st person possessive:**
`kà ín`
[≈Bible: ka in]
1POSS house
"my house"

**(182) 2nd person possessive:**
`nà ín`
[≈Bible: na in]
2POSS house
"your house"

**(183) 3rd person possessive:**
`à ín`
[≈Bible: a in]
3POSS house
"his/her house"

**Function in ZNC:**
ZNC analyzes these as POSSESSIVE markers on nouns, emphasizing their nominal function over verbal agreement.

### Singh (2018: Sukte, §8.4, pp. 218-220)

**Cognate morphemes:** kə, nə, ə

**Critical difference:** In Sukte, pronominal morphemes attach to VERBS, not nouns:
- `kə mu` "I see" (proclitic + verb)
- vs. Tedim: `ka mu` "I see" BUT also `ka inn` "my house"

**Sukte paradigm:**

| Person | Sukte | Tedim |
|--------|-------|-------|
| 1SG | kə | ka- |
| 2SG | nə | na- |
| 3 | ə | a- |

**Comparative insight:**
Proto-Kuki-Chin had *ka-, *na-, *a- pronominal morphemes with variable positioning across languages.

### Cross-Source Comparison

| Feature | Henderson | ZNC | Singh (Sukte) |
|---------|-----------|-----|---------------|
| Analysis | Agreement | Possessive | Proclitic |
| Position | Pre-verbal/pre-nominal | Pre-nominal | Pre-verbal |
| Function | Subject concord | Possession | Subject marking |
| Morphological status | Prefix | Prefix | Clitic |

## ka- (1SG/1PL.EXCL)

### Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Form | ka- |
| Category | Pronominal prefix |
| Function | 1st person singular / 1st plural exclusive |
| Sukte cognate | kə |

### Henderson (1965)

**Function:** Marks agreement with 1SG subject (kei) or 1PL.EXCL subject (ko)

**Examples:**
- `ka pai` "I go"
- `ka inn` "my house"

**Note:** Same prefix for both 1SG and 1PL.EXCL—number distinguished by context or explicit pronoun.

### ZNC (2017/2018)

**Example (181):**
`ka in`
[≈Bible: ka in]
1POSS house
"my house"

### Corpus Frequency

In the Bible corpus, ka- is extremely frequent as both possessive and agreement marker.

## a- (3)

### Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Form | a- |
| Category | Pronominal prefix |
| Function | 3rd person (singular and plural) |
| Sukte cognate | ə |

### Henderson (1965)

**Function:** Default agreement prefix for all nominals except 1st/2nd person pronouns

**Direct quote (p. 32):**
> "For all nominal forms except 'kei, keimah', &c., I, 'nang, nangmah', &c., you... concord obtains with the pronominal prefix 'a-'."

**Examples:**
- `a pai` "he/she goes"
- `a inn` "his/her house"
- `a om` "he/she exists"

### ZNC (2017/2018)

**Example (183):**
`a in`
[≈Bible: a in]
3POSS house
"his/her house"

### Special Note: Relativizer a-

In relative clauses, a- functions as a relativizer, not a 3rd person marker:
- `a pai mi` "the person who goes" (REL go person)

This is documented in ZNC's treatment of nominalization.

## i- (1PL.INCL)

### Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Form | i- |
| Category | Pronominal prefix |
| Function | 1st person plural inclusive |
| Distribution | Restricted (inclusive "we") |

### Henderson (1965)

**Function:** Marks agreement with 1PL.INCL subject (ei)

**Contrast with ka-:**
- ka-: 1SG "I" or 1PL.EXCL "we (not you)"
- i-: 1PL.INCL "we (including you)"

### ZNC (2017/2018)

ZNC documents i- as part of the inclusive/exclusive distinction typical of Tibeto-Burman.

## Directional/Object Prefixes

### hong- (TOWARD)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Form | hong- (ZNC: hɔ́ŋ-/ɔ́ŋ-) |
| Category | Directional prefix |
| Function | Toward speaker (venitive) |
| Position | Pre-verbal |

### Henderson (1965)

Henderson documents hong- as part of the directional system but details require further extraction.

### ZNC (2017/2018: §5.8.1.3)

**Function:** Goal directed toward speaker (venitive)

**Example (344):**
`van-a aksinelkai hong va lak-sak nung ci`
[≈Bible: van-a aksinelkai hong va lak-sak nung ci]
sky-LOC comet toward away take-CAUS after say.I
"They said, 'We will go and get comet from the sky'"

### Otsuka (2009)

Otsuka uses ɔ́ŋ-/óŋ- notation for this prefix.

**Function in applicatives:**
When causee/beneficiary is 1st/2nd person, hong- is obligatory:

**Example (23):**
`nu hau in kei hong-an huan-sak`
[≈Bible: nu hau in kei hong-an huan-sak]
aunt Hau ERG 1SG INV-meal cook.I-SAK
"Aunty Hau made me cook a meal" (CAUSATIVE)

---

### kong- (1→2)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Form | kong- (ZNC: kɔ́ŋ-) |
| Category | Directional/inverse prefix |
| Function | 1st person acting toward 2nd person |
| Position | Pre-verbal |

### Henderson (1965)

Henderson documents kong- in benefactive constructions.

**Example:**
`kong mat sak hi` "I've caught it for you"
[≈Bible: kong mat sak hi]

### Otsuka (2009)

Otsuka documents kong- as part of the inverse/directional system.

**Example (30):**
`ken Lian niangtui bawl-sak`
[≈Bible: ken Lian niangtui bawl-sak]
1SG.ERG Lian tea make.II-SAK
"I made some tea for Lian"

With kong- for 1→2 direction:
`kong bawl-sak hi` "I made (it) for you"

### Cross-Source Comparison

| Feature | Henderson | ZNC | Otsuka |
|---------|-----------|-----|--------|
| hong- | Directional | ɔ́ŋ-/hɔ́ŋ- "toward" | óŋ- inverse |
| kong- | 1→2 object | kɔ́ŋ- "1→toward" | 1→2 direction |
| Position | Pre-verbal | Pre-verbal | Pre-verbal |

## Synthesis: Prefix Template

### Full Prefix Slot Structure

```
[ka/na/a/i] + [kong/hong] + [va/ma] + VERB
     ↓              ↓          ↓
  Person       Direction   Motion
```

**Examples:**
- `ka pai` "I go" (1SG + go)
- `hong pai` "come" (toward + go)
- `kong piak` "give to you" (1→2 + give)
- `ka hong pai` "I come" (1SG + toward + go)

### Co-occurrence Constraints

From ZNC (p. 161):
> When both hong- and va- occur, hong- ALWAYS precedes va-.
