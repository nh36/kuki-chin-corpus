# Comparative and Augmentative Morphology in Tedim Chin

This document describes the distinction between the comparative suffix **-zaw** 
and the augmentative suffix **-pi** in Tedim Chin.

## 1. Comparative -zaw (MORE)

The suffix **-zaw** is the true comparative marker in Tedim Chin, corresponding 
to English "more X" or the comparative "-er" suffix.

### Form
- Attaches to adjectives/stative verbs
- Glossed as MORE in Leipzig notation

### Examples from corpus

| Form | Segmentation | Gloss | Translation | Corpus frequency |
|------|--------------|-------|-------------|------------------|
| hoihzaw | hoih-zaw | good-MORE | "better" | 122 |
| lianzaw | lian-zaw | big-MORE | "bigger, greater" | 98 |
| tamzaw | tam-zaw | many-MORE | "more (quantity)" | 50 |
| sangzaw | sang-zaw | high-MORE | "higher" | 18 |
| siazaw | sia-zaw | bad-MORE | "worse" | 16 |
| hatzaw | hat-zaw | strong-MORE | "stronger" | 15 |

### Corpus evidence

**Genesis 1:16** (lianzaw)
> "And God made two great lights; the **greater** light to rule the day..."

**Genesis 12:13** (hoihzaw)
> "that it may be **well** with me for thy sake" (better for me)

### Semantic function
- True comparison between entities
- Equivalent to English comparative degree
- Can be used predicatively or attributively


## 2. Augmentative -pi (AUG)

The suffix **-pi** derives from the adjective *pi* "big, great" and functions
as an augmentative marker, NOT as a true comparative.

### Form
- Attaches to nouns (primarily) and some verbs
- Glossed as AUG (augmentative) in Leipzig notation
- Often lexicalized into compounds

### Examples from corpus

| Form | Segmentation | Gloss | Translation | Corpus frequency |
|------|--------------|-------|-------------|------------------|
| khuapi | khua-pi | village-AUG | "city" | 1143 |
| kumpi | kumpi | king | "king" (lexicalized) | 1068 |
| tampi | tam-pi | many-AUG | "multitude, great many" | 733 |
| siampi | siam-pi | skilled-AUG | "priest" (lexicalized) | 364 |
| lampi | lam-pi | path-AUG | "road, highway" | 354 |
| tuipi | tui-pi | water-AUG | "sea, ocean" | 282 |
| thupi | thu-pi | word-AUG | "important word" | 115 |

### Corpus evidence

**Genesis 4:17** (khuapi)
> "and he builded a **city**"

**Genesis 10:10** (kumpi)
> "the beginning of his **kingdom**"

**Genesis 1:21** (tampi)
> "which the waters brought forth **abundantly**"

### Semantic function
- Augments size, importance, or intensity
- Often lexicalizes to form new nouns
- NOT used for comparison between entities


## 3. Key Distinctions

| Feature | -zaw (MORE) | -pi (AUG) |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Function | Comparative degree | Augmentative/intensive |
| Meaning | "more X than Y" | "big/great X" |
| Typical host | Adjectives/statives | Nouns (some verbs) |
| Lexicalization | Rare | Common |
| English equivalent | "-er, more" | "great, big, mega-" |

### Contrastive examples

- **sangzaw** (sang-zaw, high-MORE) = "higher" (comparative)
- **sangpi** (sang-pi, high-AUG) = "high wall, tall building" (augmentative)

- **lianzaw** (lian-zaw, big-MORE) = "bigger, greater" (comparative)
- **lianpi** (lexicalized) = "army, host" (augmented collective)


## 4. Note on Previous Analysis

The suffix -pi was previously glossed as COMP (comparative) in some analyses.
This is incorrect. The true comparative is -zaw. The suffix -pi is augmentative,
deriving from the adjective *pi* "big/great."

While -pi can sometimes translate as "greater" in English (e.g., "the great sea"),
this reflects augmentation of size/importance, not comparison between entities.


## 5. Related Forms

### Variant -zawk
A variant **-zawk** also appears with comparative meaning (possibly dialectal
or emphatic):
- Example: neuzawk "smaller" (freq: 10)

### Standalone zaw
The word *zaw* can also function as a standalone verb meaning "more, exceed":
- *zaw* "be more, exceed"
- Distinguished from the suffix -zaw by syntax
