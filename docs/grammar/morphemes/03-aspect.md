## Overview: Aspect vs. Tense in Tedim Chin

From ZNC (2018: §5.6, p. 147):

> "Tense and aspect are the two ways of determining time in a language which occur externally and internally. Tense operates in between various events in time and aspect is about the internal temporal structure of events (Singh 1999)."

**Key claim:**
> "In Kuki-Chin languages, aspect not tense is the dominant category for determining time." (Singh 1999, cited in ZNC)

**Grammaticalization status:**
> "Tense and aspect are not morphologically/grammatically marked in Tedim Chin. Since both are time concepts, it is expressed through various adverbial/verbal particles."

This means the "aspect suffixes" are at an intermediate grammaticalization stage—particles that function post-verbally but are not yet fully bound morphemes.

## -ta (PFV)

### Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Form | -ta (ZNC: -tà) |
| Category | Aspect suffix |
| Function | Perfective - completed action |
| Attestations | 974 |
| Sukte cognate | ta |

### ZNC (2017/2018: §5.6.1, pp. 147-148)

**Definition:**
> "The perfective -tà marks for the action that has taken place." (p. 147)

Citing Dahl & Velupillai (2013): perfective verb refers to past events.

**EXAMPLE (313):**
`u a nih in ki-kim-ta`
[≈Bible: u a nih in ki-kim-ta]
elder 3SG two CONJ REFL-equal-PFV
"Elder brother, the two are of equal size..."

**EXAMPLE (314):**
`tua nitum dek-ta in tung-thei het-lo`
[≈Bible: tua nitum dek-ta in tung-thei het-lo]
DEM dusk almost-PFV CONJ reach.I-possible.I never-NEG.I
"The sun was about to set and (she) never reached..."

### Singh (2018: Sukte)

> "The perfective aspect in Sukte is expressed by the marker ta."

**EXAMPLE (94):**
`əməʔ-in buʔ ə huən ta hi`
he-ERG rice 3 cook PFV DECL
"He cooked the rice"

### Cross-Source Comparison

| Feature | ZNC | Singh (Sukte) |
|---------|-----|---------------|
| Form | -tà | ta |
| Gloss | PFV | PFV |
| Function | Completed action | Completed action |
| Position | Post-verbal | Post-verbal |

### Open Questions

1. Does -ta stack with all other aspect markers, or are there restrictions?
2. What is the semantic distinction between -ta (PFV) and -zo (COMPL)?

## -khin (COMPL)

### Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Form | -khin (ZNC: -xín) |
| Category | Aspect suffix |
| Function | Completive - already, just now |
| Attestations | 1,013 |
| Stacks with | -ta (perfective) |

### ZNC (2017/2018: §5.6.2, pp. 147-148)

**Definition:**
> "Completed action in Tedim Chin is marked with -xín. It can be followed by the perfective marker."

**Key observation:** -khin can STACK with -ta, allowing fine-grained temporal distinctions.

**EXAMPLE (315):**
`kei pai-khin`
[≈Bible: kei pai-khin]
1SG go.I-COMPL
"I had already gone / I went"

**EXAMPLE (316) - Stacking:**
`kei pai-khin-ta`
[≈Bible: kei pai-khin-ta]
1SG go.I-COMPL-PFV
"I had already gone"

**Semantic distinction:**
- -khin: Result-state focus; emphasizes that action is complete
- -ta: Perfective; emphasizes that action has occurred (may not emphasize result)

### Cross-Source Comparison

| Feature | ZNC |
|---------|-----|
| Form | -xín |
| Gloss | COMPL |
| Function | Completed action with result emphasis |
| Stacking | Can precede -ta |

## -kik (ITER)

### Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Form | -kik (ZNC: kík) |
| Category | Aspect suffix / Habitual marker |
| Function | Iterative - again |
| Attestations | 1,632 (most frequent) |
| Etymology | < kik "to return" |

### ZNC (2017/2018: §5.6.4.4, p. 150)

**Definition:**
> "Iterative indicates repetition of an action that has already occurred before. The literal meaning of kík is 'to return'."

**EXAMPLE (321):**
`na sem kik ing`
[≈Bible: na sem kik ing]
thing do ITER 1SG.RLS
"I work again"

**EXAMPLE (322):**
`nau tangmai va en ve ci kik in`
[≈Bible: nau tangmai va en ve ci kik in]
younger cucumber away look.I IMP say.I ITER CONJ
"'Younger brother, go and see the cucumber', he said again"

## -nawn (CONT)

### Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Form | -nawn |
| Category | Aspect suffix |
| Function | Continuative - still, ongoing |
| Attestations | 1,023 |

### Corpus Evidence

**Genesis 4:12:**
`piangsak nawn lo ding hi`
[≈Bible: piangsak nawn lo ding hi]
cause.birth CONT NEG IRR DECL
"it shall not henceforth yield unto thee her strength"

### Semantic Notes

- Marks continuation of action: "still, ongoing"
- Much more frequent than -to (also CONT): 1,023 vs. 145 attestations
- Implies action continues from prior reference point

### Overview

| Attestations | 145 |

### Corpus Evidence

**Genesis 2:6:**
`ka-ai-to-in`
1SG-go-CONT-ERG
"But there went up a mist from the earth..."

### Comparison with -nawn

Both are glossed CONT but differ in frequency (145 vs. 1,023). Possible distinctions:
- -to: General ongoing action
- -nawn: "still" continuing from prior point

## Habitual Aspect System

### Overview

ZNC documents a four-way habitual distinction unique to Tedim Chin:

| Marker | Gloss | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| ngei | HAB.PST | "before but not now" |
| gige | HAB | "always" |
| zel | CONT | "continuing previous action" |
| kik | ITER | "again" |

### ngei (HAB.PST)

**ZNC (2018: §5.6.4.1):**

**EXAMPLE (318):**
`na sem ngei ing`
[≈Bible: na sem ngei ing]
thing do HAB.PST 1SG.RLS
"I used to work but not now (not anymore)"

### gige (HAB)

**ZNC (2018: §5.6.4.2):**

**EXAMPLE (319):**
`na sem gige ing`
[≈Bible: na sem gige ing]
thing do HAB 1SG.RLS
"I always work"

### zel (CONT.HAB)

**ZNC (2018: §5.6.4.3):**

**EXAMPLE (320):**
`na sem zel ing`
[≈Bible: na sem zel ing]
thing do CONT.HAB 1SG.RLS
"I continue/repeat to work"

### Cross-linguistic Note

This four-way habitual distinction is not found in Singh's Sukte grammar, suggesting possible Tedim elaboration or Sukte simplification.

## Progressive and Durative

### -lai (PROG)

**ZNC (2018: §5.6.3, p. 148):**

> "The progressive in Tedim Chin indicate that the action is in progress. It is expressed by laī."

**EXAMPLE (317):**
`van-a aksinelkai deih-lai ing ci mok`
[≈Bible: van-a aksinelkai deih-lai ing ci mok]
sky-LOC comet want-PROG 1SG.RLS say.I INTJ
"She said she still wanted comet from the sky"

**Singh (2018) Sukte cognate:**
`boŋ-in lopə ə ne lai hi`
cow-ERG grass 3 eat PROG DECL
"The cow is grazing"

### -den (DUR)

**ZNC (2018: §5.6.5, p. 150):**

> "Durative aspect emphasised about the duration of the action or event."

**EXAMPLE (323):**
`nui-den`
[≈Bible: nui-den]
laugh.I-DUR
"Laugh for a long period/time"

**EXAMPLE (324):**
`zui-den`
[≈Bible: zui-den]
follow.I-DUR
"Follow for a long period/time"
