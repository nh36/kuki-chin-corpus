# Tedim Chin Numerals

This report documents the numeral system of Tedim Chin based on analysis
of the Bible corpus (830,741 tokens).

## Overview

Tedim Chin has a decimal (base-10) numeral system with:
- Cardinal numerals (one, two, three...)
- Ordinal numerals (first, second, third...)
- Classifiers for counting specific noun types
- Distributive and multiplicative formations

## Numeral Syntax

### NUM + NOUN (attributive)

| Pattern | Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------|
| khat + N | ta khat | one child |
| nih + N | ni nih | two days |
| thum + N | kum thum | three years |

### NOUN + NUM (post-nominal)

Some numerals follow the noun:

| Pattern | Example | Meaning | Count |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| kum + NUM | kum sawm | ten years | 367 |
| ni + NUM | ni sagih | seven days | 145 |
| kha + NUM | kha khat | one month | 30 |

**Genesis 7:10**:
> A **ni sagih** ni-in leitungah tuiciin tung hi
> *3SG day seven day-CVB earth-LOC flood arrive PROX.COP*
> "after **seven days** the flood waters came"

## Distributive and Multiplicative

### Reduplication for Distribution

Numerals can be reduplicated for distributive meaning:

| Form | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| khat khat | one by one, each |
| nih nih | two by two |
| sagih sagih | seven by seven |

**Genesis 7:2**:
> **sagih sagih** pasalnu numeinu
> *seven seven male female*
> "**by sevens**, the male and his female"

### Multiplicative Expressions

| Form | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| vei khat | once |
| vei nih | twice |

## Large Numbers in Context

Biblical numerology requires large numbers:

**Genesis 5:27** (Methuselah's age):
> kum za sagih le kum sawmguk le kum kua
> *year hundred seven and year ten-six and year nine*
> "nine hundred sixty and nine years" (969)

**Genesis 11:10**:
> tuiciin khit kum nih ciangin
> *flood after year two then-CVB*
> "two years after the flood"

## Summary

1. **Base-10 system**: Units, tens (sawm), hundreds (za), thousands (sing), ten-thousands (tul)
2. **Ordinals**: -na suffix (except suppletive masa "first")
3. **Classifiers**: Human (pa/nu), occurrence (vei), amount (zat)
4. **Word order**: Flexible (NUM+NOUN or NOUN+NUM depending on context)
5. **Distribution**: Reduplication (khat khat "one by one")

**Total numeral tokens**: ~9,000+ (1.1% of corpus)

**High-frequency patterns**:
- mi khat "one person" (750x)
- kum sawm "ten years" (367x)
- ni sagih "seven days" (145x)
- za (hundred) appears 742x
