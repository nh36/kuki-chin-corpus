# Tedim Chin Relative Clauses

This report documents relative clause formation in Tedim Chin based on analysis
of the Bible corpus (830,741 tokens).

## Overview

Tedim Chin uses **head-internal** relative clauses where:
- The relativizer **a-** (3SG agreement) marks the verb
- The head noun follows the relative clause
- Nominalization with **-na** creates location/manner relatives

---

## Relative Clause Structure

### Basic Pattern

**Structure**: [[a- VERB] HEAD.NOUN]

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| a- | relativizer/agreement (3SG) |
| VERB | main predicate of relative clause |
| HEAD | the modified noun |

**Example**:
> **a bawl** mi
> *3SG make person*
> "person who makes" (lit. "he-makes person")

### Word Order

Tedim Chin relatives are **prenominal** (relative clause precedes head):

```
[REL.CLAUSE] + HEAD
[a-VERB] + NOUN
```

|------|-----------|---------|-------|

**Genesis 4:16**:
> Topa' **omna** panin Kain paikhia-in
> *LORD.POSS exist-NMLZ ABL-CVB KAIN go.out-CVB*
> "Cain went out from the **presence** of the LORD"

Nominalized forms often take locative cases:

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---------|---------|

| tenna-ah | at the dwelling-place |

## Relativization Strategies

### Subject Relativization

The subject is relativized with **a-** on the verb:

> [**a** bawl] mi
> *3SG make person*
> "person who makes" (subject = maker)

### Object Relativization

Objects may be relativized with gapping:

> [**a** ne] gah
> *3SG eat fruit*
> "fruit that (s/he) eats" (object = fruit)

### Oblique Relativization

Location/source uses nominalized forms:

> [a suah]**na** gam
> *3SG be.born-NMLZ land*
> "land where (s/he) was born"

---

## Relative Clause Position

### Prenominal (Standard)

> [[a om] lai]-ah
> *3SG exist place-LOC*
> "at the place where (it) is"

### As Complement

Relatives can serve as clausal complements:

> [[a bawl] thu] ka muh hi
> *3SG make matter 1SG see DECL*
> "I saw what he made" (lit. "I saw the made-matter")

## Summary

1. **Structure**: Head-internal, prenominal [[a-V] N]
2. **Relativizer**: a- (3SG agreement) on verb
3. **Nominalization**: -na for location/manner relatives
4. **Plurality**: -te for plural participants
5. **Position**: Relative clause precedes head noun

**Key patterns**:
- a V mi "person who V-s"
- a V lai "place where V"
- V-na "place/manner of V-ing"
- V-te "those who V"

**Frequency**:
- omna "place of being" (474x)
- muhna "sight" (247x)
- a om lai "place where exists" (133x)
