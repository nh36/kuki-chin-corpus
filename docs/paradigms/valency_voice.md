## ki- (Reflexive/Middle/Passive)

The prefix ki- has multiple related functions:

### Reflexive
Subject acts on self: *kibawl* 'make oneself'

### Reciprocal
Plural subjects act on each other: *kiten* 'hit each other'

### Middle
Subject is affected by action: *kilak* 'be taken' (from *lak* 'take')

### Passive-like
Agent defocused: *kigen* 'be said, it is said'

### Examples

**Matthew 1:11**
> Josiah ta Jekhoniah le a sanggamte hi a, Babylon gamah Israel mite salin a kipaipih hun pawlin a suak ahi uh hi.
> *Josiah ta Jekhoniah le a sanggam-te hi a Babylon gam-ah Israel mi-te sa-lin a ki-paipih hun pawl-in a suak ahi uh hi*
> JOSIAH child JEKHONIAH and 3SG brother-PL DECL 3SG BABYLON land-LOC ISRAEL person-PL meat-hope 3SG REFL-accompany time group-ERG 3SG wither be.3SG 2/3PL DECL
> KJV: *and Josias begat Jechonias and his brethren, about the time they were carried away to Babylon:*
> Target: *kipaipih* → ki-paipih → REFL-accompany

**Genesis 1:1**
> A kipat cil-in Pasian in vantung le leitung a piangsak hi.
> *A ki-pat cil-in Pasian in van-tung le lei-tung a piangsak hi*
> 3SG beginning begin-ERG PASIAN ERG sky-on and land-on 3SG cause.birth DECL
> KJV: *In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth.*
> Target: *kipat* → ki-pat → beginning

**Exodus 1:9**
> Amah in a mipihte kiangah, “En un, Israel mite eite a dingin tam lua-in thahat lua uh hi.
> *Amah in a mi-pih-te kiang-ah En un Israel mi-te eite a ding-in tam lua-in tha-hat lua uh hi*

## -sak (Causative)

The suffix -sak adds a causer argument, making intransitives transitive
and transitives ditransitive.

### Pattern
```
CAUSER + CAUSEE + VERB-sak
```

### Examples

**Matthew 1:18**
> Jesuh Khrih a hong suahna zia hih bang ahi hi. Mary pen Josef a dingin zuthawl piaksa hi a, ahi zongin a kiten taktak ma-un Kha Siangtho in amah a gaisakna thu Mary in a thei hi.

## Summary

Tedim Chin valency-changing morphology:

1. **ki-** reduces valency (reflexive, middle, passive)
2. **-sak** increases valency (causative)
3. **-pih** adds benefactive/comitative argument (applicative)
4. **-khawm** adds comitative meaning
5. **-thei** adds modal meaning (ability)

The prefix ki- is highly productive and combines with many verbs.
The causative -sak can combine with already derived forms.
