# Tedim Chin Quantifiers

This report documents the quantifier system of Tedim Chin based on analysis
of the Bible corpus (830,741 tokens).

## Overview

Tedim Chin quantifiers include:
- **Universal**: khempeuh "all", peuhpeuh "every"
- **Existential**: khat "one/some", pawlkhat "some (group)"
- **Negative**: kuamah "nobody", bangmah "nothing"
- **Degree**: tampi "much/many", tawm "few", mahmah "very"

---

## Universal Quantifiers

### khempeuh "all, every"

The primary universal quantifier, appearing post-nominally:

| Form | Gloss | Count |
|------|-------|-------|
| khempeuh | all/every | 5,191 |

**Structure**: NOUN + khempeuh

| Pattern | Meaning | Count |
|---------|---------|-------|
| mi khempeuh | all people | 1,102 |
| gam khempeuh | all lands | 170 |
| nam khempeuh | all nations | 144 |
| thu khempeuh | all things/words | 98 |

**Genesis 2:1**:
> vantung, leitung, le a sunga omte **khempeuh** kiman khin hi
> *heaven earth and 3SG inside exist-PL.POSS all complete finish DECL*
> "the heavens and the earth were finished, and **all** the host of them"

### peuhpeuh "every, each"

Distributive universal quantifier:

| Form | Gloss | Count |
|------|-------|-------|
| peuhpeuh | every/each | 455 |

Often combined with classifiers or in distributive contexts:
- "every kind" (distributive reading)
- "each one" (individuated reading)

## Existential Quantifiers

### khat "one, a, some"

Functions as both numeral "one" and indefinite article "a/some":

| Form | Gloss | Count |
|------|-------|-------|
| khat | one/a/some | 4,712 |

**As numeral**: mi khat "one person" (750x)
**As indefinite**: tapa khat "a son"

### pawlkhat "some (group)"

Refers to part of a group:

| Form | Gloss | Count |
|------|-------|-------|
| pawlkhat | some (of them) | 394 |

**Genesis 32:8**:
> "**Pawlkhat**-ah Esau pai-in a suksiat leh a **pawlkhat** suakta ding hi"
> *some-LOC ESAU go-CVB 3SG destroy if 3SG some escape IRR DECL*
> "If Esau come to the **one** company...then the **other** company shall escape"

### mi "person, someone"

Generic human reference:

| Form | Gloss | Count |
|------|-------|-------|
| mi | person/someone | 4,907 |
| mite | people | 7,235 |

## Negative Quantifiers

Formed from interrogative + emphatic **-mah**:

| Form | Structure | Meaning | Count |
|------|-----------|---------|-------|
| kuamah | kua-mah | who-EMPH = nobody | 664 |
| bangmah | bang-mah | what-EMPH = nothing | 525 |

These require negative context (negative polarity items):

**Exodus 2:12**:
> **kuamah** mu lo ahih manin
> *nobody see NEG be.3SG because-CVB*
> "there was **no man**" (lit. "nobody was seen")

See Report 6-func-04-negation.md for full analysis.

## Degree Quantifiers

### tampi "much, many"

High quantity:

| Form | Gloss | Count |
|------|-------|-------|
| tampi | much/many/very | 735 |
| tam | much | 158 |

**Genesis 17:2**:
> nang **tampi** tak kong phasak ding hi
> *2SG much very 1→2 multiply IRR DECL*
> "multiply thee exceedingly" (lit. "very **much**")

| Pattern | Meaning | Count |
|---------|---------|-------|
| mi tampi | many people | 194 |
| kum tampi | many years | 19 |
| tampi tak | very much | common |

### tawm "few, little"

Low quantity:

| Form | Gloss | Count |
|------|-------|-------|
| tawm | few/little | 118 |

**Contrast**: tampi (735x) vs. tawm (118x) shows "many" is more frequent
in Biblical narrative.

### zaw "more" (comparative)

Marks comparative degree:

| Form | Gloss | Count |
|------|-------|-------|
| zaw | more | 166 |

**Structure**: X zaw "more X"

### mahmah "very" (intensive)

Intensifier suffix:

| Form | Gloss | Count |
|------|-------|-------|
| mahmah | very/really | 1,351 |

Attaches to adjectives and quantifiers:
- tampi mahmah "very many"
- pha mahmah "very good"

## Quantifier Syntax

### Position

Most quantifiers follow the noun (post-nominal):

| Structure | Example | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| N khempeuh | mi khempeuh | all people |
| N tampi | mi tampi | many people |
| N khat | mi khat | one person |

### With Possessive

When possessor is marked:

| Structure | Example | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| POSS N khempeuh | a tate khempeuh | all his children |

### In Predicate

Quantifiers can appear predicatively:

> amaute **tampi** hi
> *they many DECL*
> "they are many"

## Summary

1. **Universal**: khempeuh "all" (5,191x) is the primary universal
2. **Existential**: khat "one/some" (4,712x) doubles as numeral
3. **Negative**: NPIs kuamah/bangmah require negative clause
4. **Degree**: tampi/tawm for quantity, mahmah for intensity
5. **Position**: Generally post-nominal

**Total quantifier tokens**: ~13,000+ (1.6% of corpus)

**Key patterns**:
- mi khempeuh "all people" (1,102x)
- mi tampi "many people" (194x)
- kuamah...lo "nobody" (negative polarity)
