# Tedim Chin Demonstratives

This report documents the demonstrative system of Tedim Chin based on analysis
of the Bible corpus (830,741 tokens).

## Overview

Tedim Chin has a two-way proximal/distal demonstrative system:
- **Proximal**: hih "this" (near speaker)
- **Distal**: tua "that" (away from speaker)

Both have plural forms and extensive use in discourse deixis.

---

## Demonstrative Inventory

### Core Forms

| Category | Singular | Plural | Corpus Count |
|----------|----------|--------|--------------|
| Proximal | hih | hihte | 5,432 / 301 |
| Distal | tua | tuate | 12,852 / 609 |

**Note**: `hi` (47,789x) is the short form of proximal demonstrative but
also functions as a sentence-final marker (see Report 10-disc-01).

### Function Summary

| Form | Gloss | Function |
|------|-------|----------|
| hih | PROX | proximal demonstrative "this" |
| tua | DIST | distal demonstrative "that" |
| hihte | PROX-PL | proximal plural "these" |
| tuate | DIST-PL | distal plural "those" |
| hi | PROX.COP | proximal copula/demonstrative |
| tu | DIST | distal (archaic/rare) |

## Demonstrative Uses

### 1. Determiner Function (DEM + NOUN)

Demonstratives precede the noun they modify:

| Pattern | Meaning | Count |
|---------|---------|-------|
| hih mi | this person | 422 |
| tua mi | that person | 272 |
| hih thu | this word/matter | 824 |
| tua thu | that word/matter | 236 |
| hih lai | this place/here | 371 |
| tua lai | that place/there | 698 |
| hih ni | this day | 16 |
| tua ni | that day | 237 |

**Exodus 32:9** (determiner):
> **Hih mite** ka mu zo hi
> *PROX person-PL 1SG see PRF PROX.COP*
> "I have seen **this people**"

**Exodus 2:21** (determiner):
> **tua mipa** kiangah om ding
> *DIST man toward exist IRR*
> "to dwell with **that man**"

### 2. Plural Demonstratives

| Form | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| hihte | hih-te | these (things/people) |
| tuate | tua-te | those (things/people) |

**Genesis 10:20**:
> **Hihte** pen a innkuan, a kam pau, a leitang
> *PROX-PL TOP 3SG family, 3SG mouth speak, 3SG land*
> "**These** are the sons of Ham, after their families"

**Genesis 7:20**:
> **tuate** tungah pi sawmnih le nih thuk-in tuumcip hi
> *DIST-PL on-LOC cubit twenty and two deep-CVB cover PROX.COP*
> "fifteen cubits upward did the waters prevail"

### 3. Pronominal Use (without noun)

Demonstratives can stand alone as pronouns:

**Genesis 48:8**:
> Israel in **hihte** a mu ciangin
> *ISRAEL ERG PROX-PL 3SG see then-CVB*
> "When Israel beheld **these**"

## Adverbial Demonstratives

### Manner Adverbials

| Pattern | Structure | Meaning | Count |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------|
| hih bangin | PROX like-CVB | "like this, thus" | 693 |
| tua bangin | DIST like-CVB | "like that, thus" | 403 |

**Genesis 6:22**:
> Pasian in gen bangin Noah in **hih bangin** bawl hi
> *PASIAN ERG speak like-CVB NOAH ERG PROX like-CVB do PROX.COP*
> "**Thus** did Noah; according to all that God commanded"

### Temporal Adverbials

| Pattern | Structure | Meaning | Count |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------|
| hih ciangin | PROX time-CVB | "at this time" | 1,531 |
| tua ciangin | DIST time-CVB | "at that time, then" | 4,305 |

**Genesis 18:10**:
> **Hih ciangin** ka hong ciahkik ding
> *PROX time-CVB 1SG 3→1 return IRR*
> "**At this time** I will return unto thee"

## Discourse Deixis

Demonstratives are heavily used for textual reference:

### Anaphoric Reference (tua)

`tua` (distal) refers to previously mentioned entities:

| Pattern | Meaning | Count |
|---------|---------|-------|
| tua ahih ciangin | "that being so, then" | 1,444 |
| tua khit ciangin | "after that, then" | 138 |

**Genesis 22:1**:
> **Tua ahih ciangin** Pasian in Abraham kuamci hi
> *DIST be.3SG then-CVB PASIAN ERG ABRAHAM test PROX.COP*
> "**And it came to pass after these things**, that God did tempt Abraham"

### Cataphoric Reference (hih)

`hih` (proximal) introduces upcoming content:

**Genesis 5:1**:
> **Hih** pen Adam suanlekhak' laipil ahi hi
> *PROX TOP ADAM descendant.POSS book be PROX.COP*
> "**This** is the book of the generations of Adam"

## Demonstrative + Copula

### hih...hi / tua...hi patterns

The sentence-final `hi` often pairs with demonstratives:

| Pattern | Function |
|---------|----------|
| hih X hi | "this is X" (equative) |
| tua X hi | "that is X" (equative) |
| X hih hi | "X is this" (identity) |

**John 1:19**:
> **Hih** pen John thusimna **ahi** hi
> *PROX TOP JOHN testimony be.3SG PROX.COP*
> "**This** is the record of John"

---

## Spatial vs. Textual Reference

| Context | Proximal (hih) | Distal (tua) |
|---------|----------------|--------------|
| Spatial | near speaker | away from speaker |
| Temporal | present/now | past/then |
| Textual | upcoming (cataphoric) | previous (anaphoric) |
| Discourse | new topic | given topic |

**Distribution in discourse**:
- `tua` (12,852x) is ~2.4x more frequent than `hih` (5,432x)
- This reflects the narrative nature of Biblical text (frequent anaphoric reference)

---

## Summary

1. **Two-way system**: proximal hih vs distal tua
2. **Number**: singular/plural distinction (-te suffix)
3. **Functions**: determiner, pronoun, adverbial
4. **Discourse**: extensive use for textual deixis
5. **High frequency**: ~19,000 demonstrative tokens (2.3% of corpus)

**Key collocations**:
- Temporal: tua ciangin "then" (4,305x), hih ciangin "now" (1,531x)
- Manner: hih bangin "thus" (693x), tua bangin "thus" (403x)
- Connective: tua ahih ciangin "that being so" (1,444x)
