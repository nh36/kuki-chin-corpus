# Tedim Chin TAM Markers

This report documents the Tense-Aspect-Mood (TAM) suffix system in Tedim Chin.

## Overview

Tedim Chin expresses tense, aspect, and mood through verbal suffixes
attached after the verb stem. Multiple TAM markers can stack.

### TAM Suffix Template

```
VERB-STEM-(DERIV)-(ASPECT)-(DIRECTIONAL)-(MODAL)
```

## Detailed TAM Analysis

### -ding (Irrealis/Future)

The irrealis marker expresses future, potential, or hypothetical events.

**Matthew 1:18**
> Jesuh Khrih a hong suahna zia hih bang ahi hi. Mary pen Josef a dingin zuthawl piaksa hi a, ahi zongin a kiten taktak ma-un Kha Siangtho in amah a gaisakna thu Mary in a thei hi.
> KJV: *Now the birth of Jesus Christ was on this wise: When as his mother Mary was espoused to Joseph, before they came together, she was found with child of the Holy Ghost.*
> *dingin*: ding-in → IRR-ERG

**Genesis 1:14**
> Pasian in, “Sun le zan a khen dingin vantungah khuavakte om hen; tuate pen pawi hun le khuahun, ni le kumte khen nading hi hen la,
> KJV: *And God said, Let there be lights in the firmament of the heaven to divide the day from the night; and let them be for signs, and for seasons, and for days, and years:*
> *dingin*: ding-in → IRR-ERG

**Exodus 1:9**
> Amah in a mipihte kiangah, “En un, Israel mite eite a dingin tam lua-in thahat lua uh hi.
> KJV: *And he said unto his people, Behold, the people of the children of Israel are more and mightier than we:*
> *dingin*: ding-in → IRR-ERG

### -ta (Perfective)

Marks completed actions, often with past time reference.

**Matthew 4:4**
> Ahi zongin Jesuh in, “Lai Siangtho sungah, 'Anlum bek tawh mite nungta lo a, Pasian genkhiat thute tawh nungta zaw hi,' ci-in kigelh hi lo ahi hiam?” ci-in a dawng hi.
> KJV: *But he answered and said, It is written, Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God.*
> *nungta*: nung-ta → life-PFV

**Genesis 1:20**
> Pasian in, “Tuite in a nungta ganhing honpite piangsak hen la, vantung kantanin leitungah vasate leeng ta hen,” ci hi.
> KJV: *And God said, Let the waters bring forth abundantly the moving creature that hath life, and fowl that may fly above the earth in the open firmament of heaven.*
> *nungta*: nung-ta → life-PFV

**Exodus 1:12**
> Ahih hangin amaute a kinenniam ciangin amaute tampi phazaw-in kizelthang zaw uh hi. Egypt mite in Israel mite kihta uh hi.
> KJV: *But the more they afflicted them, the more they multiplied and grew. And they were grieved because of the children of Israel.*
> *kihta*: kih-ta → abhor-PFV

### -zo (Completive)

Indicates action completed to its endpoint.

**Matthew 7:14**
> Ahi zongin nuntak tawntungna tun nading kong kawcikin a lampi haksa a, mi tawmno bek in a zuizo uh hi.
> KJV: *because strait is the gate, and narrow is the way, which leadeth unto life, and few there be that find it.*
> *zuizo*: zui-zo → follow-COMPL

**Genesis 14:22**
> Ahih hangin Abram in Sodom kumpipa kiangah, “Vantung le leitung a bawl Sang Pen Topa Pasian tungah ka kiciam khinzo hi.
> KJV: *And Abram said to the king of Sodom, I have lift up mine hand unto the LORD , the most high God, the possessor of heaven and earth,*
> *khinzo*: khin-zo → move-COMPL

**Exodus 1:5**
> Jakob' suanlekhakte khempeuh mi sawmsagih hi a, Josef pen Egypt-ah na om khinzo hi.
> KJV: *And all the souls that came out of the loins of Jacob were seventy souls: for Joseph was in Egypt already .*
> *khinzo*: khin-zo → move-COMPL

### -kik (Iterative/Repetitive)

Marks repeated or returned action ('again').

**Matthew 2:12**
> Herod kiangah a kihilkik loh nadingun a mang sung uhah Pasian in hilh ahih manin, lampi dang tawnin a gam uhah a ciahkik uh hi.
> KJV: *And being warned of God in a dream that they should not return to Herod, they departed into their own country another way.*
> *kihilkik*: ki-hil-kik → REFL-turn-ITER

**Genesis 3:19**
> Lei-ah na ciahkik matengin khua-ul kai-in na na sep ciang bekin an na ne ding hi. Bang hang hiam cih leh lei sung panin nang hong kipiangsak a, nang, leivui na hih manin, leivui mah na suakkik ding hi,” a ci hi.
> KJV: *in the sweat of thy face shalt thou eat bread, till thou return unto the ground; for out of it wast thou taken: for dust thou art , and unto dust shalt thou return.*
> *ciahkik*: ciah-kik → return-ITER

**Exodus 2:13**
> A zing ciangin amah paikik a, Hebru mi nih na kilai uh hi. Amah in a gamta khial mipa kiangah, “Bang hangin na lawm sat na hi hiam?” a ci hi.
> KJV: *And when he went out the second day, behold, two men of the Hebrews strove together: and he said to him that did the wrong, Wherefore smitest thou thy fellow?*
> *paikik*: pai-kik → go-ITER

### -thei (Abilitative)

Marks ability or possibility to perform an action.

**Matthew 7:21**
> “Kei kiangah, 'Topa, Topa aw,' hong ci mi khempeuh, vantung ki-ukna sungah lutthei kim lo ding uh a, vantungah a om ka Pa' deihna bangin a gamtateng bek lutthei ding uh hi.
> KJV: *Not every one that saith unto me, Lord, Lord, shall enter into the kingdom of heaven; but he that doeth the will of my Father which is in heaven.*
> *lutthei*: lut-thei → enter-ABIL

**Genesis 40:8**
> Amaute in ama kiangah, “Mang ka man uh a, tuate a khiathei ding om lo hi,” na ci uh hi. Josef in amaute kiangah, “Mang khiat theihnate Pasian' hong piak hi lo ahi hiam? Kei kiangah hong gen un, hong thum ing,” a ci hi.
> KJV: *And they said unto him, We have dreamed a dream, and there is no interpreter of it. And Joseph said unto them, Do not interpretations belong to God? tell me them , I pray you.*
> *khiathei*: khiat-thei → interpret-ABIL

**Leviticus 10:9**
> “Nang hitaleh na tapate hitaleh kikhopna bukah na pai uh ciangin leenggahzu hitaleh khamtheih zu hitaleh ne kei un. Na nek uhleh na si ding uh hi. Tua pen na suan na khakte uh tungah khang tawntungin ngeina kip khat hi ding hi.
> KJV: *Do not drink wine nor strong drink, thou, nor thy sons with thee, when ye go into the tabernacle of the congregation, lest ye die: it shall be a statute for ever throughout your generations:*
> *khamtheih*: kham-theih → forbid-ABIL

## TAM Suffix Stacking

Multiple TAM markers can combine on a single verb:

| Pattern | Example | Gloss |
|---------|---------|-------|
| VERB-ITER-PFV | pai-kik-ta | go-ITER-PFV 'went again' |
| VERB-COMPL-IRR | bawl-zo-ding | make-COMPL-IRR 'will finish making' |
| VERB-out-PFV | khia-ta | go.out-PFV 'went out' |
| VERB-ABIL-IRR | bawl-thei-ding | make-ABIL-IRR 'will be able to make' |

## Summary

Tedim Chin TAM system features:

1. **Core aspectual markers**: -ta (PFV), -zo (COMPL), -kik (ITER)
2. **Modal markers**: -ding (IRR), -thei (ABIL)
3. **Continuative/Prospective**: -nawn (CONT), -lai (PROSP)
4. **Stacking**: Multiple markers can combine in fixed order
