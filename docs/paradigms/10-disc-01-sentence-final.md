# Tedim Chin Sentence-Final Particles

This report documents the sentence-final particle system of Tedim Chin based
on analysis of the Bible corpus (830,741 tokens).

## Overview

Tedim Chin uses sentence-final particles to mark:
- **Mood**: declarative, interrogative, jussive, exclamative
- **Aspect**: perfective, resultative
- **Force**: imperative, hortative

The most frequent sentence-final marker is **hi** (24,754 verse-final occurrences).

## Declarative: hi

The particle **hi** marks conclusive, assertive statements. It is by far the
most common sentence-final element.

### Basic Function

| Pattern | Structure | Meaning | Count |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------|
| V hi | VERB DECL | "V (conclusive)" | many |
| ahi hi | 3SG.COP DECL | "it is" | 4,494 |
| lo hi | NEG DECL | "is not" | 1,695 |
| ding hi | IRR DECL | "will be" | 7,042 |
| uh hi | 2/3PL DECL | "they (do)" | 11,377 |
| ta hi | PFV DECL | "has become" | 577 |
| zo hi | PRF DECL | "has finished" | 338 |

### Examples

**Genesis 1:13** (simple declarative):
> ni thum ni ahi **hi**
> *day three day be.3SG DECL*
> "the third day"

**Genesis 4:5** (negative declarative):
> thusim lo **hi**
> *accept NEG DECL*
> "he had not respect"

### hi as Copula vs. Sentence-Final

`hi` functions both as:
1. **Copula**: "be" (when preceded by NP/ADJ)
2. **Sentence-final**: declarative marker (clause-finally)

These often combine: **ahi hi** = "3SG-be DECL" = "it is"

## Interrogative: hiam

The particle **hiam** marks questions (both yes/no and content questions).

| Pattern | Meaning | Count |
|---------|---------|-------|
| V hiam | "V?" | 5,230 |
| ahi hiam | "is it?" | common |
| bang hiam | "what?" | 21 |

**Genesis 48:8**:
> "Hihte kua ahi **hiam**?"
> *PROX-PL who be.3SG Q*
> "Who are these?"

## Jussive/Optative: tahen, hen

### tahen "let it be"

Marks jussive mood (third-person commands/wishes):

| Pattern | Meaning | Count |
|---------|---------|-------|
| V tahen | "let V" | 230 |
| hi tahen | "let it be so" | common |

**Genesis 9:25**:
> "Kanaan in samsiatna ngah ta hen la, a sanggamte' sila hi **tahen**"
> *CANAAN ERG curse obtain PFV OPT and 3SG sibling.POSS slave be.3SG JUSS*
> "Cursed be Canaan; a servant of servants shall he be"

### hen "let"

Marks optative mood (wishes, permissions):

| Pattern | Meaning | Count |
|---------|---------|-------|
| V hen | "may V" | 670 |
| ngah ta hen | "may receive" | common |

**Genesis 1:3**:
> Khuavak om ta **hen**
> *light exist PFV OPT*
> "Let there be light"

## Exclamative: aw

Marks exclamative utterances, often in vocatives or emphatic contexts:

| Pattern | Meaning | Count |
|---------|---------|-------|
| N aw | "O N!" | 2,306 |
| lungdamna aw | "O joy!" | common |

**Psalm 100:1**:
> Gam khempeuh **aw**, Topa' tungah lungdamna aw gingsak un
> *land all EXCL LORD.POSS on-LOC joy voice raise IMP.PL*
> "Make a joyful noise unto the LORD, all ye lands"

Note: `aw` also functions as a vocative marker in address.

## Particle Combinations

Multiple particles can stack:

| Combination | Structure | Meaning |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| ahi hi | COP DECL | "it is (conclusive)" |
| ding hi | IRR DECL | "will (conclusive)" |
| ta hi | PFV DECL | "has become" |
| uh hi | 2/3PL DECL | "they (did)" |
| lo uh hi | NEG 2/3PL DECL | "they did not" |

**Order**: VERB + (ASP) + (AGR) + (NEG) + (MOOD)

## Summary

1. **Declarative hi**: Most frequent (24,754 verse-final), marks conclusive assertions
2. **Interrogative hiam**: Question marker (5,230x total)
3. **Jussive tahen**: Third-person commands, "let it be" (230x)
4. **Optative hen**: Wishes and permissions (670x)
5. **Imperative in/un**: Singular/plural commands
6. **Exclamative aw**: Emphasis, vocative (2,306x)
7. **Aspectual ta/zo**: Perfective/completive aspect

**Key patterns**:
- ahi hi "it is" (4,494x)
- ding hi "will be" (7,042x)
- uh hi "they (did)" (11,377x)
- lo hi "is not" (1,695x)
