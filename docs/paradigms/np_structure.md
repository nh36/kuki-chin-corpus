# Tedim Chin Noun Phrase Structure

This report documents the structural patterns of noun phrases (NPs) in Tedim Chin,
based on analysis of the Bible corpus.

## Overview

Tedim Chin noun phrase structure follows a general template:

```
(POSS-GEN) (DEM) HEAD (PROP) (NUM) (QUANT) (-PL) (CASE)
```

Key characteristics:
- **Head-initial for modifiers**: Adjectives, numerals, and quantifiers FOLLOW the head noun
- **Demonstratives are prenominal**: Demonstratives PRECEDE the head noun
- **No numeral classifiers**: Nouns directly precede numerals
- **Plural -te**: Attaches to head noun or quantifier
- **Case markers close the NP**: ERG, LOC, COM, ABL, etc.

## Summary: NP Structure Template

```
Full NP template:

(POSS-GEN) (DEM) HEAD-N (-PL) (PROP) (NUM) (QUANT) (CASE)

Examples filling different slots:

                          mi                                  = person
                          mi-te                               = people
              hih         mi-te                               = these people
              hih         mi-te          khempeuh             = all these people
              hih         mi-te          khempeuh      in     = all these people (ERG)
mite'                     tung                          -ah   = on the people
              tua         pa                    khat          = that one man
```
