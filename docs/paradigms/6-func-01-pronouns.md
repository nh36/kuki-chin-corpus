# Tedim Chin Pronouns

This report documents the pronoun system of Tedim Chin based on analysis
of the Bible corpus (830,741 tokens).

## Overview

Tedim Chin has a rich pronoun system with the following distinctions:
- **Person**: 1st, 2nd, 3rd
- **Number**: singular, plural
- **Clusivity**: 1st person distinguishes inclusive (kote) vs exclusive (eite)
- **Emphasis**: plain vs emphatic (-mah suffix)
- **Function**: independent, possessive (prefixal), emphatic

## Pronoun Functions

### 1. Subject Pronouns

Independent pronouns occur in subject position:

**Genesis 1:27** (3SG subject)
> Tua ahih ciangin Pasian in **ama** lim bangin mihing piangsakin
> *that be.3SG then-CVB PASIAN ERG 3SG image like-CVB person cause.birth-CVB*
> "So God created man in **his** own image"

**Genesis 24:7** (1SG subject)
> Ka pa' inn le **ka** suahna leitang panin **kei** hong
> *1SG father.POSS house and 1SG birth-NMLZ land ABL-CVB 1SG.PRO 3→1*
> "The LORD...which took **me** from **my** father's house"

### 2. Possessive Prefixes

Possessive prefixes attach directly to nouns:

| Form | Structure | Gloss | Example |
|------|-----------|-------|---------|
| ka pa | ka-pa | 1SG-father | "my father" |
| na nu | na-nu | 2SG-mother | "your mother" |
| a ta | a-ta | 3SG-child | "his/her child" |
| kan inn | kan-inn | 1PL-house | "our house" |
| nan gam | nan-gam | 2PL-land | "your land" |
| an topa | an-topa | 3PL-lord | "their lord" |

**Genesis 20:13**
> Pasian in **ka pa'** inn panin hong paikhiatsak
> *PASIAN ERG 1SG father.POSS house ABL-CVB 3→1 cause.go.out*
> "God caused me to wander from **my father's** house"

### 3. Object Pronouns

Independent pronouns also serve as objects (no distinct object forms):

**Genesis 3:11** (1SG object)
> kua in hong gen **kei** tungah
> *who ERG 3→1 speak 1SG.PRO on-LOC*
> "Who told **thee**"

### 4. Emphatic Pronouns

The -mah suffix adds emphasis or contrast:

**Genesis 17:9** (2SG emphatic)
> **Nangmah** le na suanlekhak-te'n ka thukham uh
> *2SG.EMPH and 2SG descendant-PL.POSS.ERG 1SG covenant 2/3PL*
> "**Thou** shalt keep my covenant, thou and thy seed"

## Clusivity Distinction

Tedim Chin distinguishes inclusive vs exclusive 1st person plural:

| Pronoun | Type | Meaning | Count |
|---------|------|---------|-------|
| kote | inclusive | we (including hearer) | 888 |
| eite | exclusive | we (excluding hearer) | 794 |
| eimah | excl. emphatic | we ourselves (not you) | 3 |

**Example of exclusive** (speaker's group, hearer excluded):
> **Eite** kiangah hong gawm in
> "Come near to **us** (exclusive)"

**Example of inclusive** (speaker and hearer together):
> **Kote** pai ding
> "**We** (inclusive) will go"

**Negative indefinites** use interrogative + -mah:
> **kuamah** theih lo
> "**nobody** knows"

> **bangmah** om lo
> "**nothing** exists"

## Directional/Inverse Markers

Related to pronouns but not pronouns themselves, these markers indicate
the direction of action relative to speech act participants:

| Marker | Gloss | Direction | Count |
|--------|-------|-----------|-------|
| hong | 3→1 | towards 1st person | 15,578 |
| kong | 1→2 | from 1st to 2nd | 2,005 |

**hong** (action towards speaker):
> Topa in **hong** piak
> "The LORD **gave (to me/us)**"

**kong** (1st person acting on 2nd):
> Nang **kong** huh ding
> "I will **help (you)**"

## Verbal Agreement Markers

These suffixes agree with subjects but are distinct from pronouns:

| Suffix | Gloss | Function | Count |
|--------|-------|----------|-------|
| -uh | 2/3PL | 2nd/3rd person plural | 31,321 |
| -un | PL.IMP | plural imperative | 9,667 |
| -ing | 1SG | 1st singular declarative | 22,822 |
| -eng | 2SG | 2nd singular | 1,954 |

**Examples**:
> pai **uh** "they go" (3PL subject)
> pai **un** "go!" (2PL imperative)
> ka pai **ing** "I go" (1SG declarative)

## Summary

1. **Person/number/clusivity**: Full 7-way distinction (1SG, 2SG, 3SG, 1PL.INCL, 1PL.EXCL, 2PL, 3PL)
2. **Emphasis**: -mah suffix for emphatic forms
3. **Possession**: Prefixal possessives (ka-, na-, a-, kan-, nan-, an-)
4. **Reflexive**: ki- verbal prefix, not a pronoun
5. **Indefinites**: Interrogative + -mah for negative indefinites
6. **Case**: No distinct accusative forms; same form for subject and object

**Total pronoun tokens**: ~63,000+ (7.6% of corpus)
