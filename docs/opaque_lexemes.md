# Opaque Lexemes in Tedim Chin

This document catalogs words that superficially appear to be compounds (they can be
segmented into two or more known morphemes) but are actually **opaque lexemes** where
the compositional meaning does not transparently relate to the actual meaning.

These words should NOT be analyzed as compounds in the morphological analyzer. They
are treated as indivisible stems with idiosyncratic meanings.

## Criteria for Opacity

A word is considered an opaque lexeme when:
1. It can be segmented into two or more known morphemes
2. Each putative morpheme has a clear meaning in other contexts  
3. BUT the combination of those meanings does not plausibly yield the actual meaning
4. The word is likely a historical compound that has become lexicalized/fossilized

## Confirmed Opaque Lexemes

### sanggam 'brother'
- **Apparent analysis:** sang (high/early) + gam (land)
- **Compositional meaning:** "high-land" or "early-land"
- **Actual meaning:** brother (kinship term)
- **Evidence:** Used consistently for 'brother' (Cain/Abel story, Gen 4)
- **Verdict:** No semantic connection. Opaque lexeme.

### lungno 'worm'
- **Apparent analysis:** lung (heart) + no (young/small)
- **Compositional meaning:** "heart-young" or "small heart"
- **Actual meaning:** worm (the creature)
- **Evidence:** Job 25:6, Ps 22:6, Isa 51:8, Jonah 4:7 - all clearly "worm"
- **Note:** Distinct from lungnop (lung-nop = heart-rest = peace/desire)
- **Verdict:** No semantic connection. Opaque lexeme.

### luanghawm 'carcass'
- **Apparent analysis:** luang (flow) + hawm (counsel/hollow)
- **Compositional meaning:** "flow-counsel" (?)
- **Actual meaning:** carcass, corpse, dead body
- **Evidence:** Lev 26:30 "cast your carcasses upon the carcasses of your idols"
- **Verdict:** No semantic connection. Opaque lexeme.

### singtawng 'stock' (wooden idol)
- **Apparent analysis:** sing (tree/wood) + tawng (false)
- **Compositional meaning:** "tree-false" or "wood-lie"
- **Actual meaning:** wooden stock/idol (thing worshipped)
- **Evidence:** Jer 2:27 "saying to a stock, Thou art my father"
- **Note:** Could be transparent ("false wood" = idol), but analysis uncertain
- **Verdict:** Possibly opaque. Treated as stem for safety.

### numei 'woman/female'
- **Apparent analysis:** nu (woman/mother) + mei (fire)
- **Compositional meaning:** "woman-fire" (?)
- **Actual meaning:** woman, female
- **Evidence:** Gen 1:27 "male and female created he them"
- **Note:** Core vocabulary. Etymology unclear - may be historical compound.
- **Resolution:** mei here is NOT 'fire' but a separate Tibeto-Burman root *mei 
  meaning 'female'. This is a widespread TB cognate. nu-mei = mother-female → woman.
- **Verdict:** TRANSPARENT compound once mei 'female' is recognized.

## Tibeto-Burman Cognates

### mei 'female' (TB *mei)
There is a root `mei` meaning 'female' that is homophonous with `mei` 'fire' but 
etymologically distinct. This is a widespread Tibeto-Burman cognate found in many
related languages.

**Compounds using mei 'female':**
- **numei** = nu (mother) + mei (female) → "woman" ✓
- **meigong** = mei (female) + gong (alone) → "widow" (female-alone) ✓

This root has been added to the analyzer's atomic stems with appropriate documentation.

### lo 'field' (distinct from lo 'NEG')
There is a root `lo` meaning 'field' that is homophonous with `lo` 'NEG' (negative marker).

**Compounds using lo 'field':**
- **lopa** = lo (field) + pa (man) → "farmer" ✓
- **lokhawh** = lo (field) + khawh (work) → "farming" ✓

### hawm 'hollow/enclosure' (distinct from hawm 'counsel')
There is a root `hawm` with a secondary meaning 'hollow/enclosed space' used in:
- **suanghawm** = suang (stone) + hawm (hollow) → "cave" ✓

## Notes on Methodology

When deciding whether a word is an opaque lexeme:

1. **Check multiple contexts** - Use KJV cross-reference to verify the meaning
2. **Consider all possible decompositions** - Sometimes a different split point works
3. **Look for metaphorical extensions** - Some compounds have metaphorical meanings
4. **Consider historical compounds** - Words may have become opaque over time
5. **Look for cultural connections** - Some compounds are transparent within cultural context
6. **When in doubt, don't decompose** - It's better to leave a word unanalyzed than to give a misleading analysis

## Implementation in Analyzer

Opaque lexemes should be:
1. Listed in NOUN_STEMS or VERB_STEMS as indivisible units
2. NOT listed in BINARY_COMPOUNDS or TERNARY_COMPOUNDS
3. NOT decomposable by the algorithmic suffix/prefix parsing

Semantically questionable compounds (like numei, meigong) remain in BINARY_COMPOUNDS
with holistic glosses. This allows morphological decomposition while preserving
semantic accuracy.

The analyzer respects the principle: **explicit entries take precedence over algorithmic decomposition**.
