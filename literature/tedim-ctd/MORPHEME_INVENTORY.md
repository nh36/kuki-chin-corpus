# Tedim Chin Morpheme Inventory

Based on analysis of the Tedim Bible corpus (31,000+ verses) and cross-referenced with Henderson 1965 and VanBik 2009.

**Current Analyzer Performance:**
- **Overall Coverage: 92.8%** (771,935 / 831,408 tokens)
- Best: John 95.0%, Genesis 94.5%
- Average: 92-93% across most books

**Corpus Statistics:**
- Total verses analyzed: 31,105
- Unique word forms: ~22,000
- Most frequent function words: a (58k), hi (36k), in (23k), uh (22k), ding (19k)

## 14. Notes on Henderson 1965 Cross-Reference

Henderson's *Tiddim Chin: A Descriptive Analysis of Two Texts* (1965) provides the foundational grammatical description. Key correspondences:

### 14.1 Phonology
- Henderson notes tonal distinctions (high, low, falling)
- Final -h in orthography often indicates tone
- Glottal stop marked by apostrophe (')

### 14.2 Verbal Morphology
- Henderson describes the ka-/na-/a- paradigm as "pronominal prefixes"
- The kong-/hong- system is analyzed as "transitive with 1st person object"
- Aspect markers (ding, ta, zo) are described as "verbal suffixes"

### 14.3 Stem Alternation (Stem I vs Stem II)
According to Henderson and VanBik 2009, some verbs show stem alternation:
- Stem I: basic/realis form
- Stem II: modified form (used with certain TAM markers)

Examples (to be verified against corpus):
| Stem I | Stem II | Gloss |
|--------|---------|-------|
| mu | muh | see |
| za | zak | hear |
| thei | theih | know |
| ngai | ngaih | listen |

**Note**: The corpus shows both forms occurring (mu: 962, muh: 501; thei: 2,543, theih: 1,383), confirming stem alternation is productive.

### 14.4 Clause-Final Position
Henderson describes the sentence-final hi as a "declarative particle" rather than a copula. This aligns with its high frequency (35,961) and consistent clause-final position.
