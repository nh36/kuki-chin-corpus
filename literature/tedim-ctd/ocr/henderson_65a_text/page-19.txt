Pre-nuclear phonematic umts. The phonetic information avail-
able for the establishment of the pre-nuclear elements of syllable
structure can most conveniently be referred to in the vocabulary
at the end of this study. As has always been stressed, the material
so far assembled is too incomplete for a systematic study of the
relative distribution of initial consonants and following vowels and
consonants to be made. In the absence of such a study it is futile
to attempt to draw up an exhaustive inventory of pre-nuclear
phonematic units. The best that can be done is to draw attention
to some of the factors which examination of the present material
suggests require to be taken into account when such an inventory
comes to be made.

Mention has already been made of the possible desirability of
abstracting certain phonological features of syllable-onset (see
pp. 22-23). Clearly any such abstraction would be likely. to affect
the number and nature of the pre-nuclear elements. Further
clues as to their nature might be found in the distribution of
accompanying back and front modification (see p. 22).

(palatal), and W (labio-velar).
