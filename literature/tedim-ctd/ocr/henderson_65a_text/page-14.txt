Close front, close rounded back, and open central to back vowels,
i, u, a, are found both long and short in stressed syllables, Half-
close front and half-close back rounded vowels e, o are found short
only, while half-open front and half-open back rounded vowels are
always long, except before the glottal stop, €1, 01, £7, 9°.

Diphthongs may start from any vowel position and move
towards either a close front or close back rounded vowel, ei, ex,
ai, ati, eu, emu, au, amu, &c., or they may start from close vowel
positions and move towards a central open vowel, ia, ua.

The triphthongs are of what may be termed. the ‘opening-
closing’ type, that is to say they start with a close vowel position,
move to a more open vowel position, and then proceed to a closer
vowel position. In the two commonest, uai, iau, there is an overall
movement from back to front or front to back, but iai and uau
are also found. ;

For a more detailed description of the pronunciation of vowels,
diphthongs, and triphthongs see pp. 11-13.
