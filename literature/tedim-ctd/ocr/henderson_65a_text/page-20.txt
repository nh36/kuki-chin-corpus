THE NARRATIVE STYLE

1. SENTENCES

THE largest structure selected for analysis is the sentence.

A sentence is, for the purpose of this analysis, a structure con-
stituted of appropriate placed and unplaced elements, as defined
and described in the analysis.

The unplaced elements of sentence structure are certain proper-
ties which characterize the sentence as a whole, namely:

A. The nature, number, and sequence of the placed elements.
B. Sentence intonation.
C. Mood,

D. Pronominal concord within the sentence.

The placed elements of sentence structure are phrases and sen-
tence particles,

A. The nature, number, and sequence of the placed
elements
Phrases

represented in the structural formulae by S, P, and A respectively.
