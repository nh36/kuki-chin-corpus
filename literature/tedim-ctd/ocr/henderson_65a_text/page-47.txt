Derived verbs

A few verbs which appear at first sight to be irregular may be
regarded as derived from other verbs and hence as in a class of
their own. Form I of the derived verb is identical with Form IT
of the original verb. ,

Compare, for instance, ~dam to be well, with a regular Form II
sdam, and dam to heal (tr.). Both Form I and Form I of the
latter verb are sdam, whereas we should expect dap for Form II
in a regular verb (see p. 77).
