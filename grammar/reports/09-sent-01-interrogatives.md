# Tedim Chin Interrogatives

This report documents the interrogative system of Tedim Chin based on analysis
of the Bible corpus (830,741 tokens).

## Overview

Tedim Chin distinguishes two main question types:
- **Yes/no questions**: Marked by sentence-final particle **hiam**
- **Content questions**: Use question words (bang "what", kua "who", etc.)

---

## Question Particle: hiam

The particle **hiam** marks yes/no questions and appears sentence-finally:

| Form | Function | Count |
|------|----------|-------|
| hiam | interrogative marker | 5,230 |

**Structure**: CLAUSE + hiam

**Genesis 4:6**:
> Bang hangin nang heh a, bang hangin na mai sia ahi **hiam**?
> *what for 2SG angry and what for 2SG face bad be.3SG Q*
> "Why art thou wroth? and why is thy countenance fallen?"

### hiam with Content Questions

When content questions use explicit wh-words, **hiam** still appears:

**Genesis 48:8**:
> "Hihte kua ahi **hiam**?"
> *PROX-PL who be.3SG Q*
> "Who are **these**?"

## Question Formation Patterns

### Yes/No Questions

**Structure**: [CLAUSE] + hiam

| Example | Gloss | Translation |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| om hiam | exist Q | "is there?" |
| pha hiam | good Q | "is it good?" |
| thei hiam | know Q | "do you know?" |

**Genesis 24:23**:
> Inn-ah hong tum theih na hiam?
> *house-LOC 3→1 stay know.NMLZ Q*
> "Is there room for us to lodge?"

### Content Questions

**Structure**: [WH-word] + ... + hiam

| Pattern | Meaning | Count |
|---------|---------|-------|
| bang ahi hiam | what is it? | 56 |
| kua ahi hiam | who is it? | 33 |
| bang hiam | what? | 21 |
| om hiam | is there? | 11 |
| kua hiam | who? | 6 |

### Rhetorical Questions

Often use **bang** without expecting an answer:

**Genesis 3:20**:
> **Bang hang** hiam cih leh amah pen mihing khempeuh' nu ahi hi
> *what for Q say if 3SG TOP human all.POSS mother be.3SG PROX.COP*
> "**Why**? Because she was the mother of all living"

---

## Embedded Questions

Questions can be embedded in larger clauses:

**Indirect question**:
> ... bang hiam cih thei lo uh hi
> *... what Q that know NEG 2/3PL PROX.COP*
> "they knew not **what** it was"

**Verb**: thei "know" takes indirect question complements

## Summary

1. **Yes/no questions**: Marked by sentence-final **hiam**
2. **Content questions**: WH-word + ... + hiam
3. **Main WH-words**: bang "what", kua "who", bangci "how", banghangin "why"
4. **Position**: Question particle is always clause-final
5. **Embedding**: Questions embed with retained question words

**Total interrogative tokens**: ~10,000+ (hiam + WH-words)

**Key patterns**:
- hiam (5,230x) - main question marker
- bang "what" (4,263x) - most common WH-word
- banghangin "why" (451x) - frequent in Biblical rhetoric
